Julia Gehrlein

Research Associate in High Energy Theory

Brookhaven National Laboratory

I am a theoretical particle physicist interested in new physics scenarios. My work focuses on the most elusive particles of the Universe: Neutrinos. I am currently a research associate at the High Energy Theory group at Brookhaven National Laboratory. I did my PhD at the Instituo de Fisica Teorica UAM-CSIC in Madrid, Spain, where I was funded by the Marie Curie ITN “elusives”.

Interests

  • All things neutrino! Including neutrino models, new physics searches at neutrino experiments, connection of neutrinos to other open questions of the Standard Model.
